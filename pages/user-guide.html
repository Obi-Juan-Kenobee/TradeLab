User Guide

Getting Started

Welcome to TradeLab! This guide will help you make the most of our trading journal platform.

Quick Start

  1. Navigate to the Manual Entry page to start logging your trades
  2. Use the Import Trades feature to bulk import your trading history
  3. View your performance in the Analytics section

Manual Trade Entry

Record your trades individually using our intuitive trade entry form:

  • Symbol: Enter the trading symbol (e.g., AAPL, MSFT)
  • Market: Specify the market (e.g., Stocks, Crypto)
  • Entry Price: Your buy-in price
  • Exit Price: Your selling price
  • Quantity: Number of shares/units traded
  • Date: When the trade was executed
  • Notes: Add any relevant trade notes or strategy used

Importing Trades

Import multiple trades at once from your Excel/CSV files:

  1. Prepare your spreadsheet with the required columns using our template found here.
  2. Click "Choose File" to select your spreadsheet
  3. Map your columns to our trade fields
  4. Click "Import" to process your trades

Supported Formats: .xlsx, .xls, .csv

*Supported brokers: Fidelity, TD Ameritrade and Robinhood.

If you want your broker supported please contact us. If you can, download a sample template from your broker.

Analytics

Track your trading performance with our comprehensive analytics:

  • Win Rate: Percentage of profitable trades
  • Profit/Loss: Total and average P/L metrics
  • Trade History: Visual representation of your trading activity
  • Performance by Market: See which markets you perform best in

Market Insights

Stay informed with market data from TradingView:

  • Market overview across different asset classes
  • Top gainers and losers
  • Economic calendar
  • Cryptocurrency market data

Tips & Best Practices

  • Our template works best, but if you can't copy the data to our template, we recommend renaming the columns to match ours for easier importing. While this is not necessary, it makes it easier for us to process your trades. We currently support Fidelity, TD Ameritrade (Schwab) and Robinhood.
  • Add detailed notes to learn from your trades
  • Review your analytics weekly to identify patterns
  • Use the import feature for historical trades
